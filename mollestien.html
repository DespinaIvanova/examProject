The Scandinavian street

What I believe is interesting about Møllestien is that the place is located in one of the most mainstream areas in the city of Aarhus but it is still hidden and secluded. You might ask ‘What kind of place is Møllestien?’ - well the way I would describe is a tiny cozy street on which you can find the most ‘hyggelig’ (comes from ‘hygge’) Scandinavian houses. I love the street because of its charm and the fact that once you step there you can immediately sense Scandinavia.

I remember being so excited to see the Scandinavian architecture, the little houses which scream DENMARK. Once I found the street, I felt completely settled in the city as now I was aware of the existence of the place that would let me embrace Scandinavia as it is - minimalistic and cozy. If Scandinavia was a color it would be a warm color, perhaps beige. However, the houses on Møllestien are far from just beige - colorful houses which contribute to the overall charm of this narrow street. The day I decided to visit the place, the sun was shining and I felt like the happiest person in the world. It is crazy how the weather can affect your mood.

It is a rare occurrence to have the sun up in December in Denmark that is why I took advantage of the fact and went on a stroll. First, I took 1A from Aarhus S (southern Aarhus) and when I arrived downtown I took a five-minute walk to Møllestien which is near a canal and a park, called Mølleparken. I forgot to mention that before getting to the street I went to my favorite little bakery which was on my way to Møllestien. The bakery is called David’s Breadhead and it’s the cutest bakery I have been to in the city. Of course, I treated myself with a good old cardamom bun (very Danish) and a chocolate croissant. Now, that I had the pastries with me I was ready to be captivated by Scandinavia.

While strolling, enjoying the place and of course the most delicious cardamom bun for a split second I felt like one of those Scandinavian girls, enjoying herself while surrounded by what I would call the most Scandinavian place of all.
